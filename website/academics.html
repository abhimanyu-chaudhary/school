Academics

Syllabus Plan & Curriculum Levels

We offer a balanced academic curriculum integrating regional, national (NEB/SEE), and modern tech learning formats to ensure comprehensive student growth.

Early Childhood Foundation (KG - UKG)

We focus on learning-by-doing methods, alphabet phonetics, counting numbers up to 100, shape drawings, coloring activities, and sensory motor coordination check-ins.

  • English Phonics & Early Vocabulary building.
  • Basic Nepali Letters (Barahakhari, Aksharmala).
  • Mathematics: Counting, grouping, simple shapes, patterns.
  • Creative Arts: Crafting, music, rhyme recitations, and storytelling.

Lower Primary Curriculum (Grade 1 - 3)

Transitions students into formal writing, structured reading comprehension, arithmetic calculations, environmental studies, and basic ICT skills.

  • Languages: Core reading/grammar classes in English and Nepali.
  • Mathematics: Multi-digit addition, subtraction, and introductory multiplication.
  • Science & Society: My family, my community, hygiene, and regional geography.
  • Computer Education: Parts of a computer, basic paint drawing, keyboard usage.

Upper Primary Curriculum (Grade 4 & 5)

Expands on scientific analysis, mathematical word problems, historical timelines of Nepal, and writing compositions.

  • Mathematics: Fractions, decimals, angles, perimeter, and area calculations.
  • Science: Human body systems, plants and animals classification, and matter states.
  • Social Studies: History of Nepal, civic responsibilities, and map drawing.
  • ICT & Coding: Typing proficiency, MS Word layouts, and basic scratch coding modules.

Middle School Curriculum (Grade 6 - 8)

Prepares students with core logical and numerical skills in algebra, physics/chemistry basics, and structural essays.

  • Mathematics: Algebra, geometry theorems, statistics, and sets.
  • Science: Physics (force, pressure), Chemistry (elements, formulas), Biology, and Astronomy.
  • Social Studies & Geography: World geography, cultural heritage, and natural resources of Nepal.
  • Computer Programming: HTML layouts, CSS styling, and logic puzzles.

Secondary Board Curriculum (Grade 9 & 10)

Structured strictly under the Nepal National Examination Board (NEB) rules, preparing students to excel in their Secondary Education Examination (SEE).

  • Languages: Compulsory English, Compulsory Nepali (grammar, literature).
  • Sciences: Compulsory Science, Compulsory Mathematics, and Optional Mathematics.
  • Social Science: Compulsory Social Studies, Health, Population, & Environment (HPE).
  • Technical Elective: Computer Science (QBasic coding, database queries, and hardware theory).

Rules & Code of Conduct

Shishu Gyan Nikunja maintains high discipline standards to ensure a safe, clean, and collaborative boarding ecosystem.

1. Attendance & Punctuality

Students must achieve at least 85% attendance to qualify for terminal exams. Late arrivals are checked at the security gate, and consecutive check-ins will trigger notice alerts to parents.

2. Electronic Devices & Mobiles

Personal smart devices, cell phones, and gaming consoles are strictly prohibited on the school campus and in boarding dorms. Laptops used for curriculum coding are checked and monitored by the ICT warden.

3. Uniform & Grooming

Students must wear clean, ironed school uniforms with polished black shoes. Hair must be neatly groomed, and nails must be kept trimmed.

4. Boarding House Conduct

Boarders must follow the daily timetable strictly (study hours, dining schedules, lights out). Outings are only allowed on parents' written requests or during official vacations.
